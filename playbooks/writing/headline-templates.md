# Headline Templates

## Purpose
Strong headlines grab attention and accurately represent the content. Use these templates as starting points, then customize for your specific interview.

## Template Categories

### The "How" Headline
Focuses on process, method, or approach.

**Templates:**
- How [Person] [Achieved/Built/Created] [Thing]
- How [Person] [Action] [Result] in [Timeframe]
- How [Person] Overcame [Challenge] to [Achievement]

**Examples:**
- How Sarah Built a $1M Business in 18 Months
- How John Transformed His Company Culture
- How Maria Overcame Imposter Syndrome to Launch Her Startup

### The "Why" Headline
Focuses on motivation, reasoning, or insights.

**Templates:**
- Why [Person] [Action] and What They Learned
- Why [Person] Believes [Controversial/Interesting Statement]
- Why [Person] Left [Thing] to Pursue [Thing]

**Examples:**
- Why Tom Quit His Corporate Job to Start a Nonprofit
- Why Lisa Believes Remote Work Is the Future
- Why David Left Tech to Become a Teacher

### The "Story" Headline
Focuses on narrative or journey.

**Templates:**
- The [Adjective] Story of [Person] Who [Action]
- From [Starting Point] to [End Point]: [Person]'s Journey
- How [Person] Went From [Situation A] to [Situation B]

**Examples:**
- The Inspiring Story of a Refugee Who Built a Tech Company
- From Corporate Executive to Social Entrepreneur: Jane's Journey
- How Mike Went From Bankruptcy to Building a Multi-Million Dollar Business

### The "Insight" Headline
Focuses on lessons, wisdom, or key takeaways.

**Templates:**
- [Number] Lessons from [Person] on [Topic]
- What [Person] Learned from [Experience]
- [Person]'s [Number] Rules for [Topic]

**Examples:**
- 5 Lessons from a Serial Entrepreneur on Building Teams
- What Emma Learned from Scaling Her Business to 100 Employees
- Mark's 3 Rules for Sustainable Growth

### The "Question" Headline
Poses an intriguing question.

**Templates:**
- Can [Person] [Achievement/Goal]?
- What Happens When [Person] [Action]?
- Is [Person]'s Approach to [Topic] the Future?

**Examples:**
- Can This Founder Change How We Think About Work?
- What Happens When a CEO Prioritizes Employee Wellbeing?
- Is Remote-First the Future of Business?

### The "Contrarian" Headline
Challenges conventional wisdom.

**Templates:**
- Why [Person] Thinks [Common Belief] Is Wrong
- [Person]'s Unconventional Approach to [Topic]
- The [Topic] Advice [Person] Wishes They'd Ignored

**Examples:**
- Why This CEO Thinks Work-Life Balance Is a Myth
- Sarah's Unconventional Approach to Hiring
- The Startup Advice Tom Wishes He'd Ignored

### The "Number" Headline
Uses specific numbers or statistics.

**Templates:**
- How [Person] [Achievement] in [Number] [Timeframe]
- [Person]'s [Number]-Year Journey to [Achievement]
- [Number] Things [Person] Wishes They Knew About [Topic]

**Examples:**
- How Anna Built a $5M Company in 3 Years
- John's 10-Year Journey to IPO
- 7 Things This Founder Wishes They Knew About Fundraising

## Headline Best Practices

### Do's
- **Be specific:** Include concrete details
- **Use active voice:** Stronger and more engaging
- **Include the person's name:** Personal connection
- **Promise value:** What will the reader learn?
- **Keep it accurate:** Don't oversell the content
- **Test multiple options:** Write 5-10 variations

### Don'ts
- **Avoid clickbait:** Don't mislead readers
- **Don't be vague:** "Interesting Interview" is weak
- **Avoid jargon:** Use accessible language
- **Don't overpromise:** Match the headline to content
- **Avoid clich√©s:** "Game-changer," "revolutionary," etc.

## Headline Testing

### Questions to Ask
1. Does it accurately represent the content?
2. Would I click on this headline?
3. Is it specific enough?
4. Does it promise clear value?
5. Is it too long or too short? (50-60 characters ideal for SEO)

### A/B Testing
- Write 3-5 headline variations
- Test with a small audience
- Use the one that performs best
- Consider the platform (LinkedIn vs. Twitter vs. blog)

## Platform-Specific Considerations

### Blog/Website
- Can be longer (60-70 characters)
- Focus on SEO keywords
- Can be more descriptive

### Social Media
- Shorter is better (40-50 characters)
- More emotional/punchy
- Include emojis if appropriate for brand

### Email
- Subject line length matters (50 characters)
- Focus on curiosity and value
- Avoid spam trigger words
